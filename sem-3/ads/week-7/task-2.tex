Since, for a ring homomorphism \(f\)
\begin{displaymath}
    f(a + b) = f(a) +' f(b)
\end{displaymath}
must hold, we can say that
\begin{displaymath}
    \begin{aligned}
        f(x + 0) &= f(x) +' f(0) & \implies \\
        f(x) &= f(x) +' f(0) & \implies \\
        f(0) &= 0'
    \end{aligned}
\end{displaymath}
